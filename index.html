Projects

Rock Paper Scissors SVM

As part of my junior design course in the Robotic Musicianship Lab, another student and I were tasked with building a system that can use supervised learning to watch a game of rock paper scissors via ultrasound imagery and determine the winner. To do this, we attached ultrasound probes to our forearms and trained two copies of a supervised learning model using Max MSP and SKLearn's support vector classifier. We then counted down a timer that sent between two Windows machines using UDP, and we played rock paper scissors, where a live image would send from both probes to separate systems, which then predicted whether a hand was holding rock, paper, or scissors, then both systems would share their result to determine a winner.
